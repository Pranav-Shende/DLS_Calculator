What is DLS?

In cricket, DLS stands for the Duckworth–Lewis–Stern method.

It is a mathematical method used to recalculate targets and results in limited-overs matches (ODI/T20) that are interrupted, usually by rain or bad light. It works on the idea that a batting team has two key “resources”:

  • Overs (or balls) remaining
  • Wickets in hand
